SPF Mistakes Women Over 35 Make

So, let’s talk about the SPF mistakes I see women over 35 making — and yeah, I’ve been guilty of a few myself! Not applying enough sunscreen is a huge one. Infuriating, right? We think we’re protected, but we’re actually missing out on key benefits. Another mistake? Not reapplying throughout the day. I’ve had days where I felt “good enough” and skipped it. Spoiler alert: the sun doesn’t care about your intentions! These mistakes can seriously sabotage your anti-aging efforts, so let’s clear things up.

How I Learned to Apply Enough Sunscreen

Here’s a fun fact: Most people don’t use enough sunscreen. The recommended amount for your face alone is about a nickel-sized dollop. I’d apply just a little dab, thinking that’d do the trick. Nope! If you’re not covering every inch, you’re essentially wearing a sun filter with holes. To get better at this, I started using a pump bottle because it forces me to use more. Who knew such a small thing could make a big difference?

Why Reapplying SPF Is Non-Negotiable

Reapplying every two hours? Yeah, it’s a must. So, what was I doing? I’d slather it on in the morning and then forget about it. I thought, “Hey, I’m inside, I’m safe!” — which is totally wrong. UV rays can still get to you through windows. If you're outside, sweat and water can wash it off too. Carrying a travel-size SPF or a spray version helps. Trust me, you’ll thank yourself later.

How I Choose the Right SPF for My Skin Type

SPF is not one-size-fits-all. I struggled with this for ages. Not every sunscreen is great for every skin type. If you’ve got oily skin, go for a gel formulation that won’t clog pores. Dry skin? Cream-based is better. Sensitive to things? Look for one with physical UV filters like zinc oxide. Know your skin, and it’ll guide you to the right product.

Checklist: How to Perfect Your SPF Game

  • Use at least a nickel-sized amount for your face.
  • Apply sunscreen 15 minutes before sun exposure.
  • Reapply every two hours, or more often if you’re sweating or swimming.
  • Choose SPF 30 or higher for daily use.
  • Find a formula that fits your skin type.
  • Don’t skip your neck and ears; they need love too!

FAQs About SPF and Anti-Aging

1. Does using SPF really help with aging?

Absolutely! Sunscreen protects your skin from UV rays that cause premature aging, so it’s a must in your anti-aging routine.

2. Can I use makeup with SPF instead of sunscreen?

Makeup with SPF is great but shouldn’t replace your regular sunscreen. Layers work better for protection!

3. What’s the best time to apply sunscreen?

Apply it as the last step in your morning skincare routine. If you’re going outside, do it about 15 minutes beforehand.
