How to Read a Skincare Ingredient List

Reading a skincare ingredient list can feel like deciphering a secret code. But it doesn’t have to be. First off, look for the INCI (International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients) names, which are usually in Latin or chemical terms. The first few ingredients are the most important since they make up the bulk of the product. And don’t stress if you can’t pronounce everything. It’s more about knowing what works for your skin type!

How I Tackle Ingredient Lists

Here’s how I break it down when I’m checking out a new product:

  • Focus on the first five ingredients. They define the product.
  • Scan for known irritants, like alcohols or fragrances, especially if you have sensitive skin.
  • Look for active ingredients that your skin needs, like hyaluronic acid for hydration or retinol for anti-aging.
  • Check the preservatives. Not all are bad, but some can be sketchy.
  • Understand the functions of common terms like “emollient” (moisturizing) or “exfoliator.”

What Ingredients Should I Avoid?

We’ve all made the mistake of falling in love with a product only to realize it’s not for us. I bought a moisturizer once that had so much alcohol, my face felt like a desert! So, here are some ingredients I'd suggest you keep an eye out for:

  • Alcohol Denat: Can dry you out.
  • Fragrance: Can irritate sensitive skin.
  • Parabens: Often avoided for skin safety.

How to Find Ingredients For Your Skin Type

It’s all about knowing your skin and what it craves. If you’re oily, look for products with salicylic acid. Dry skin? Go for rich oils or ceramides. Sensitive? Soothing ingredients like aloe or chamomile are your go-tos. Always remember, even the most popular products can have ingredients that your skin might not like. I still cringe thinking about how a well-known brand broke me out!

Skincare Routine by Ingredient Type

Here’s a quick checklist for building your routine based on ingredient types:

  • Cleansers: Look for gentle surfactants.
  • Toners: Choose hydrating toners with no alcohol.
  • Serums: Pick based on your target (anti-aging, brightening).
  • Moisturizers: For dry skin, choose heavier creams; for oily skin, opt for gels.
  • Sunscreen: Always use broad-spectrum protection with at least SPF 30.

FAQ

  • What’s the INCI reading guide? It’s a standardized way to name ingredients, which helps you understand what’s really in your products.
  • Why do some ingredients have weird names? Most come from Latin or are technical names. Don’t worry about that! Focus on the effect they have on your skin.
  • Do I need to read every ingredient list? It’s ideal for products you use daily. For occasional products, a quick scan can be enough!
