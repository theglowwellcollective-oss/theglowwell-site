Why You Need SPF Indoors

So, you’re inside, right? You might think you can skip the sunscreen, but that’s a big mistake. Even indoors, UV rays can sneak through windows and damage your skin, leading to aging and other issues. I used to think SPF was just for beach days, but here’s the truth: if you want to keep that glow and prevent premature wrinkles, you’ve got to wear your indoor SPF every day.

How Do Indoor UV Rays Work?

Alright, let's break it down. There are two types of UV rays we need to worry about: UVA and UVB. UVA rays can penetrate through windows. They contribute to aging and skin damage. UVB rays, on the other hand, are mostly responsible for sunburn, and you’re right — they don’t have much of an effect indoors. But ignoring UVA is a recipe for disaster!

How I Picked the Right Indoor SPF

Investing in the right indoor SPF was totally worth it for me. I used to grab whatever was on sale. Major mistake! Make sure to look for a broad-spectrum sunscreen that protects against both UVA and UVB. It should feel good on your skin too, so you’re not feeling greasy or weighed down. I love ones with light textures that don’t interfere with my makeup.

So, How Do You Use SPF Indoors?

Applying SPF should be part of your daily routine. Here’s my quick checklist to make it easy:

  • Choose a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher.
  • Apply a generous amount—about a nickel-sized dollop for your face.
  • Don't forget areas like your neck and ears!
  • Reapply every two hours, especially if you’re near a window.
  • Make it part of your morning routine after moisturizer.

What About Cloudy Days?

Honestly, I used to think I could get away without SPF when the sun was hiding. But even on cloudy days, up to 80% of UV rays can still reach your skin! So yes, sunscreen is non-negotiable, rain or shine!

Common Questions About Indoor SPF

1. Can I use my regular moisturizer instead of sunscreen?

Not really! Most moisturizers don’t have enough SPF. You need dedicated sunscreen for proper protection.

2. Is SPF really necessary if I’m not outside all day?

You bet! Like I said, indoor UV rays can still cause damage, so don’t skip it even if you’re chilling.

3. What if I wear makeup? Do I really need SPF?

Yep! You can use makeup with SPF, but make sure it’s broad-spectrum and use a proper layer of sunscreen underneath. You can also find sprays for easy reapplication!
