How to Transition Your Skincare Routine for Fall

Changing your skincare routine for fall is super important. As the weather gets cooler, your skin needs different care. Think richer moisturizers, fewer exfoliants, and more hydrating products. Seriously, if you keep using your summer routine, your skin might end up dry or flaky — not cute.

What’s the best way to switch up your products?

First things first, check the ingredients in your summer products. If they’re light and watery, like gel moisturizers, it’s time to swap them out. Fall calls for thicker creams that lock in moisture. I usually choose something heavier with nourishing oils or butters. But don’t be afraid to experiment a little because it might take a few tries to find what your skin loves in cooler weather.

How I adjust my cleansing routine

For cleansing, I like to ditch my foaming cleansers if my skin starts feeling tight. Instead, I reach for a creamier, hydrating cleanser. Cream cleansers are gentler and help keep your skin barrier intact during those chilly months. Always remember to cleanse twice a day but make it less harsh and more soothing.

Steps for a successful seasonal skincare routine change

Here’s a quick checklist to guide your fall skincare transition:

  • Switch to a thicker moisturizer.
  • Look for hydrating ingredients like hyaluronic acid or aloe.
  • Use a gentler cleanser.
  • Incorporate a nourishing face oil, if you want!
  • Limit exfoliation to once a week, especially if your skin is sensitive.
  • Don’t forget SPF — yes, even in fall!

When should I start transitioning my routine?

Honestly, it can be a little tricky to know the exact moment to switch. I usually start this transition in late September. If you feel your skin getting tight or look in the mirror and see dry patches, it’s probably time. Just listen to what your skin's telling you!

Common questions about seasonal skincare routine change

What ingredients should I look for in fall products?

Look for nourishing ingredients like shea butter, ceramides, and oils like jojoba or almond. They keep your skin hydrated and protected.

Can I still use my summer products?

You can, but adjust how often you use them and consider mixing in fall-friendly products. It’s all about balance!

How do I know if a product’s right for my skin in fall?

Try a patch test before fully committing. If it feels heavy or breaks you out, it might not be the right fit.
